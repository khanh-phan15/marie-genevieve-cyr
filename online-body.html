This interplay between the digital and physical object reflects a larger shift in spectatorship, where the secondary viewing experience (often online) becomes as significant, if not more so, than direct engagement with the physical object.

The idea of performance and performativity also extends beyond the live realm into digital spaces, with live-streaming, selfies, and documentation becoming integral parts of the performance of identity.
